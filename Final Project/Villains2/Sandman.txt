Sandman was created by [[Stan Lee]] and Steve Ditko.\n==Appearances==\n\n===[[Marvel Cinematic Universe]]===\n====''[[Spider-Man: Far From Home]]''====\nA Sandman who is a technologically-created decoy appears in the movie as part of [[Mysterio]]'s scheme\n\n====''[[Spider-Man: No Way Home]]''====\nA Sandman who is an actual mutated human will appear in this movie.\n\n===''[[Ultimate Spider-Man]]''===\n====Early life====\nFlint Marko was a criminal, when one day he escaped from prison, but unfortunately was caught up in an atomic explosion. The explosion had mutated Flint into sand particles and was enable to control them. Later, [[Nick Fury]] captured him and left him on an isolated island at the Bermuda Triangle. He became lonely at the island and created a playmate for himself, which he called \"Sandy\".\n\n====Present====\nFlint was visited one day by [[Spider-Man]] and the other [[S.H.I.E.L.D.|trainees]], who were on vacation. He decided to play with them, but the trainees escaped with the plane from his dangerous actions. Unnoticed by Spider-Man and the others, Sandman hid inside Spider-Man's unitard. He was noticed by them and they landed the plane on the river that was encased in ice. Sandman tried to get on land to destroy Manhattan, but was stopped by [[Sam Alexander|Nova]], who heated his silica in the sand to glass.\n\nS.H.I.E.L.D. put him in a special hourglass prison that stopped his reformation with vibrations.\n\n===''[[Spider-Man (TV series)|Spider-Man]]''===\nSandman appears in the new series ''Spider-Man'' with his daughter [[Keemia]] who also has sand powers.\n\n==Trivia==\n*''[[Ultimate Spider-Man]]'' and ''[[Spider-Man (TV series)|Spider-Man]]'' are Sandman's fourth and fifth animated appearance.
