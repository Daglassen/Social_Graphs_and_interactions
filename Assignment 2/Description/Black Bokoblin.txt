They are much more resilient and are usually armed with stronger {{Plural|BotW|Weapon}} than their common counterparts, including {{Plural|BotW|Dragonbone Boko Club|link}} and {{Plural|BotW|Dragonbone Boko Bat|link}}. {{Plural|BotW|Black Bokoblin}} may be found in monster camps and occasionally on roads where they may attack passing travelers. {{Plural|BotW|Black Bokoblin}} may also sometimes be found going on hunts while riding {{Plural|BotW|Horse|link}}.\n\nBlack Bokoblins share an attack pattern with other {{Term|BotW|Bokoblin}} counterparts. On the ground, {{Plural|BotW|Black Bokoblin}} mainly attack by swinging and slamming their held {{Plural|BotW|Weapon}}. In the event that their {{Plural|BotW|Weapon}} is lost or stolen, they will search for any other available {{Plural|BotW|Weapon}} nearby and use them instead. In some monster camps, {{Plural|BotW|Black Bokoblin}} may also be found standing on top watch towers, where they may alert other monsters to {{Term|BotW|Link}}'s presence by blowing a horn. These {{Plural|BotW|Black Bokoblin}} may also be equipped with {{Plural|BotW|Bow|link}} that they use to snipe intruders with {{Plural|BotW|Arrow|link}}.\n\nWhen defeated, {{Plural|BotW|Black Bokoblin}} may drop {{Plural|BotW|Bokoblin Fang|link}}, {{Plural|BotW|Bokoblin Horn|link}}, or {{Term|BotW|Bokoblin Guts|link}}.\n\n==={{StBotW|-}}===\n{{Future Release}}\n{{Stub|StBotW}}\n{{Plural|StBotW|Black Bokoblin}} are set to appear in {{StBotW|-}}.\n\n==Other Appearances==\n==={{CoH|-}}===\n{{Ambig|Start}}\n{{Stub|CoH}}\n[[File:CoH Black Bokoblin Sprite.png|left]]\n{{Plural|CoH|Black Bokoblin}} appear as enemies in {{CoH}} where they move to the beat of the music.
