''[[The Story of Robin Hood and His Merrie Men]]''.\n\n==Role in the film==\nRed Gill is first seen entering an archery tournament at [[Nottingham (1952 location)|Nottingham]] fair organized by [[Prince John (1952 character)|Prince John]] and his new [[Sheriff of Nottingham (1952 character)|Sheriff of Nottingham]] in order to choose which archer will join and serve them in gathering taxes from the poor in [[King Richard (1952 character)|King Richard]]'s absence.\n\nAccording to the Sheriff, Red Gill is among the finest archer he has ever recruited to join them until his archery is outwitted and outmatched by [[Robin Hood (1952 character)|Robin Fitzooth]] and his father, [[Hugh Fitzooth]] even when Red Gill mocks Robin after shooting his arrow and before Robin and his father shoot theirs.\n\nKnowing the Sheriff's true intentions, after Hugh Fitzooth and his son defy the Sheriff and refuse to join him, the Sheriff sends Red Gill to kill them. In the [[Sherwood Forest (1952 location)|forest]], Red Gill manages to kill Hugh Fitzooth with an arrow shot on the back. Luckily, Robin manages to avenge his father's death by killing Red Gill with an arrow shot on his chest when Red Gill tries killing him next.
